Embracing Equality

International Women's Day (IWD) is celebrated annually on March 8 to commemorate the cultural, political, and socioeconomic achievements of women. It's a day to recognize women's achievements, raise awareness about women's equality, and highlight the ongoing struggle for gender parity worldwide.

The day also marks a call to action for accelerating gender balance. The theme for International Women's Day 2025 focuses on innovation for gender equality, recognizing the potential of technological advancements to create a more equitable future for all genders.

The History of Women's Day

International Women's Day has been observed for over a century, with the first gathering held in 1911. Here's how it evolved over the years:

1908

The Beginning

15,000 women marched through New York City demanding shorter working hours, better pay, and the right to vote.

1911

First International Women's Day

Following the decision of the International Women's Conference, International Women's Day was honored for the first time in Austria, Denmark, Germany, and Switzerland on March 19.

1913

Date Changed to March 8

International Women's Day was transferred to March 8, and it has been celebrated on this day ever since.

1975

United Nations Recognition

The United Nations began celebrating International Women's Day during International Women's Year.

2000

Millennium Development Goals

The UN included gender equality as one of the eight Millennium Development Goals to be achieved by 2015.

2025

Innovation for Equality

Today, International Women's Day belongs to all groups collectively everywhere. It's not country, group, or organization specific, but a global day celebrating the social, economic, cultural, and political achievements of women.
